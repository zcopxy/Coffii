TL;DR

GO — but as a craft & marketing play, not a gold rush

Build a fast, private, genuinely beautiful espresso-only shot log for the home barista. The wedge isn't a missing feature — it's taste and focus. Every pull auto-generates a gorgeous, shareable "Shot in Review" card that doubles as the product's payoff, its viral loop, and our entire faceless-content engine.

The honest part: this niche has no hated incumbent to dethrone (the leaders are well-loved) and it's already crowded with 2025–26 clones. The realistic ceiling is ~€1k/mo, maybe €4k on a good run — real cashflow, not life-changing money. We win by out-crafting soulless clones and out-marketing them with consistent faceless content, not by exploiting a gap. It's a grind with a modest, achievable payoff that we'd actually enjoy because we both love coffee.

01The strategy this came from

The genre: find an iOS app that's trending or growing, has notably bad reviews, build an ~80% version that fixes the top complaint, and market it faceless ("the [category] app that finally fixes X"). Modest goal, two people, one-to-two-week build. It fits our DNA: privacy-first, ASO/SEO-first, on-device, no team.

We scored candidates against four tests: trending, hated incumbent to displace, clonable fast, not yet crowded. Espresso was the early front-runner from a market lens. Then we went deep — and the picture changed in an important way (next section).

Other candidates we looked at and dropped: an aquarium/reef water tracker (great on paper — genuine unmet demand, ugly dated incumbents — but killed on personal relevance: neither of us owns an aquarium, and you can't make months of faceless content about something you don't care about). Also considered: posture app, recipe organizer, whisky cellar log, and a "swing for the fences" honest AI calorie counter.

02The honest reality (read this twice)

Deep research flipped two of our four assumptions. We're keeping this front and centre so we go in with eyes open.

TestVerdictDetail
Trending / growingPASSProsumer machines + specialty-coffee-at-home are genuinely growing.
Hated incumbent to displaceFAILLeaders are well-rated: Beanconqueror 4.9, Filtru 4.8, iBrewCoffee 4.7. Complaints are minor UX nits, not "this is a scam." No villain to beat.
Clonable in 1–2 weeksPASSThe core logger, yes. The hard-20% (BLE scales, AI coach) is what blows the budget — so we cut it from v1.
Not already crowdedFAILIt's an active gold rush: ~15+ shot loggers, many shipped in 2025–26, mostly converging on the same feature list. We'd be entrant ~#15, not a first mover.
What this means

The game is not "exploit an obvious gap a hated app left open" — that gap doesn't exist here. The game is "out-craft a crowd of generic clones and out-market them." That's a legitimate way to win a small niche, but it's a polish-and-distribution grind, and distribution (not code) is the real work. Nobody in this niche is rich — the category leader makes an estimated $5–6k/mo.

03Why we do it anyway

  • Genuine personal relevance. We're daily espresso drinkers and actually care about dialing in. That's the single biggest predictor of whether we'll sustain months of content — and it killed every "better on paper" candidate that bored us.
  • Biggest market + best content material of the shortlist. Far more home baristas than reef-keepers, strong search volume, and espresso is gorgeous on video (more on that below).
  • Clean DNA fit. Local-first, no accounts, no data collection, on-device — exactly how we like to build, and a real differentiator in a niche where users care about owning their data.
  • The reframed game is winnable. Indie devs in this niche converge on features; almost none have taste. Taste + focus + a built-in sharing loop is a position the crowd hasn't taken.

We're not trying to dethrone Beanconqueror's power users. We're taking the slice the dense, intimidating incumbents repel: the person who just bought a machine and wants to get good without reading a manual.

04The edge: design-led + the Shot Card loop

Be honest about what design is and isn't. A nice UI is copyable — it's not a moat. But in this situation it's the most valuable thing we can have, because of what it unlocks.

The core insight

Every time you pull a shot, the app auto-generates a beautiful, shareable "Shot in Review" card — the bean, the dial (dose → yield, ratio, time), grind, a taste note, an optional photo, our watermark. That one mechanic is four things at once:

The card is…Why it matters
Product payoffThe satisfying moment after logging — the reason to open the app every single day.
Viral loopEvery card someone shares is a branded ad with our name on it.
Content engineIt's our recurring TikTok/Reels format — we can generate these endlessly without filming ourselves.
ASO heroThe card is the App Store screenshot. The product is the ad.

Two card types, one engine

  • Standard "Shot in Review" — beautiful from data alone (a beginner who didn't take a photo still gets a gorgeous card). Photo is an optional enhancement, never a gate.
  • The diff card — our secret weapon. "Dialed it in: 1:2.5 / 22s / sour → 1:2 / 30s / 🎯." Shows a win, not just a stat. This is the one coffee nerds actually share on r/espresso.

Design discipline (so we don't blow the budget)

  • One signature animation — a stylized pour filling a cup, or the shot-timer ring closing — used on the card reveal. Avoid the fluid-simulation tarpit; a clipped shape + gradient mask "reads" as liquid without the physics. Time-box it to a day.
  • Calm everywhere else. Warm palette, custom iconography (your "almost-emoji-but-tasteful" instinct), generous spacing, micro-interactions, haptics on shot start/stop. Calm is the aesthetic — over-animating reads cheap.
  • Aim it at the beginner-to-intermediate prosumer — the bigger, softer, less-defended segment. Hardcore nerds buy on data depth and won't switch for beauty; that's fine, they're not our customer.

06The product (concept level)

The core loop is one sentence: last good pull → what changed → next shot, logged in ~10 seconds, ending in a card. Everything serves that. (We're deliberately not doing screen-by-screen here — that's the build doc.)

✓ In for v1

  • Local-first (SwiftData), no account
  • Beans, grinder, machine, basket profiles
  • Fast shot log: dose→yield, ratio, time, grind, temp; advanced fields hidden by default
  • Live shot timer + haptics
  • One-tap re-log last shot
  • The Shot Card + the diff card
  • Pinned "next shot" note + "last good pull"
  • Per-bean history + a few charts
  • Bean-resting reminders
  • CSV/JSON export (their data, freely)

✗ Deferred (the hard-20%)

  • Bluetooth scale integration (Acaia / Bookoo) — biggest time sink + permanent QA burden
  • AI dial-in coach — adds backend + cost + credibility risk; do it later as a paid "+" tier
  • Community / social feed
  • Full iCloud sync (start local; add later)
  • Pour-over / other brew methods — stay espresso-pure

07The marketing engine (faceless, no aesthetic setup needed)

The unlock

"My setup isn't pretty enough for TikTok" is a non-issue. The best faceless coffee content is tight macro — the stream tiger-striping out of a naked portafilter, crema swirling, the first drip. Real footage, shows no face, no kitchen, no setup — just four inches around the cup. A ~€30 phone macro lens + a clean dark background is the whole kit.

Three content lanes

  1. Macro pours — real, gorgeous, anonymous. Carries our technical credibility.
  2. App screen-recordings — the animations + the card reveal. This is where the product sells itself.
  3. AI imagery — vibe only. Caution: coffee people instantly clock fake crema / impossible pours. Use AI for ambient/background/mood, never for anything that implies "we know coffee." Let real macro carry the authority.

Formats & channels

  • Repeatable formats: diagnose-this-shot (sour/bitter/channeling), before→after dial-in, myth-busting, satisfying timer/card reveal — all with the card as the through-line and CTA.
  • Channels: r/espresso (respect the self-promo rules), Espresso Aficionados Discord for beta credibility, TikTok/Reels for reach, problem-first SEO pages ("why is my espresso sour", ratio calculator) → install.
  • Don't lead with "AI." Lead with the result and the beauty.
Expectation-set

Treat user-shared cards as upside, not the plan. The reliable engine is our own posting cadence. Design the card so organic sharing is frictionless — and if it catches, great — but bet on our consistency, not on strangers posting unprompted.

08Money

Pricing model

  • Freemium core, with a pay-once Pro: €19.99 launch / €24.99 standard (lifetime).
  • Annual €14.99 and monthly €2.49 offered, but not the hero — this audience resents subscriptions for a logging tool.
  • Never paywall their own data or export. Trust is the brand.
  • An "AI+" tier can come later, once the coach exists and earns its keep.

The reality math

Pay-once €19.99 nets roughly ~€14 after VAT (~21%) and Apple's 15% (Small Business Program). So:

TargetNet sales / moInstalls / mo needed (~3% buy)
€1,000 / mo~70 sales~2,300
€4,000 / mo~285 sales~9,500

This is a cashflow business, not MRR — pay-once means we must keep acquiring. That's exactly why the content engine has to be sustainable and the card loop has to pull its weight. €1k/mo is a plausible, satisfying outcome for a weekend project; €4k is the optimistic-but-real top end.

09Guardrails

App Review

  • Guideline 4.3 (duplicate functionality) is the top risk — clear it with our own name, icon, UI, and at least one visibly distinct feature (the card loop does this).
  • 4.1 (copycats) — don't ape anyone's look.
  • 2.3.7 (metadata) — never put a competitor's name in our App Store listing. (We can bid on their brand via Apple Search Ads.)

Comparative marketing

  • In TikTok captions we can name a competitor only with currently-true, verifiable facts — never their logo/screenshots, never the word "scam." Nominative fair use + EU comparative-advertising rules.

Privacy (our brand)

  • No accounts, no analytics SDKs that phone home, no data collection. "Your shots never leave your phone" is both true and a selling point.

10How we build it

  • Stack: SwiftUI + SwiftData, local-first, optional iCloud later. iOS-first.
  • Speed: AI agentic coding makes the clean CRUD app cheap and fast — realistically a working v1 in the 1–2 week window if we hold scope.
  • The mindset that matters: the build was never the bottleneck. Distribution is. Every hour we'd spend gold-plating a feature is better spent on the card design and the first 30 pieces of content.
If you read one line

Ship a small, beautiful, focused thing fast — then spend the real effort marketing it consistently for months. The app is the easy part.

11Open questions & next steps

Decisions to make together

  • Name. Working ideas: Crema, Pull, Dialed, Ristretto, Tamp, Shot, Demitasse, Naked (portafilter pun). Needs to be App-Store-searchable and not taken.
  • Freemium line: what's free vs Pro? (Proposal: unlimited logging free; Pro = charts, diff cards, custom card themes, export.)
  • Card aesthetic direction — one default theme we both love before anything else.
  • Who owns what — code split, content split, posting cadence.

What's explicitly deferred

BLE scales, AI coach, community, full iCloud sync, non-espresso brew methods. All are "earn it later," not v1.

Immediate next step

If we're both in: lock the name + freemium line, then produce the build spec (screen-by-screen + the signature animation) and a content starter kit (the repeatable formats + first 10 hooks). The strategy work is done; the rest is deciding and doing.

Gut-check for the partner

This is a modest, eyes-open bet: small niche, no easy gap, ~€1–4k/mo ceiling, won on craft + consistent marketing rather than a clever exploit. The upside is it's genuinely fun for two coffee people, cheap to build, and the card loop gives us a real shot at the content compounding. If "fun + a few hundred to a few thousand a month + great portfolio piece" is worth a couple of weekends + a marketing habit — it's a go.
